# Thoughts of my life

![boat in ocean](/blog/img/boat-in-the-ocean.jpg)

This year, ever since I started my new job, life has gradually become busier. The number of visitors to my clinic keeps increasing, and with every shift, I feel more mentally and physically drained by the time I get off work.

I know exercise is important for restoring energy and maintaining balance. However, the more time I spend working shifts, the fewer opportunities I have to exercise regularly. Because of that, I have come to realize that the second most important thing for maintaining energy is having a healthy and balanced diet. Fewer sugary drinks, less processed food, and fewer unhealthy additives — these small choices probably play a big role in helping us stay healthy.

For the past few weeks, I have repeatedly fallen into periods of depression and emotional exhaustion. Sometimes I do not even want to go outside or begin a new project. During moments like these, one thing that helps me stay positive is reading books on my e-book reader. I am especially drawn to motivational, spiritual, and personal life experience genres. Among the books I have read, the one that resonates with me the most so far is ≪I May Be Wrong≫.

Recently, I accidentally came to realize something: the more intelligent human beings become, the easier it is for the mind to grow vicious and arrogant. Ego quietly steals away our conscience, humility, carefulness, and kindness if we do not slow down and reflect on ourselves amid aggressive social interactions.

Many times, I need to speak to myself honestly, repent from time to time, and look inward. I remind myself:

> “You are not better than others.
> You are simply fulfilling the work God has entrusted to you.”

And the same is true for everyone else — convenience store staff, gas station workers, delivery drivers, office workers, accountants, and countless ordinary people. Every person is carrying their own responsibilities in this circle of life.

At the same time, I also remind myself that I am not worse than people who seem more successful than me. Life moves in seasons and opportunities. When the right opportunity arrives, we simply need to prepare ourselves well enough to catch the fate-changing ball when it comes.

I think it is good to start writing a journal every day. Sometimes, writing quietly becomes a way of understanding ourselves a little better.
