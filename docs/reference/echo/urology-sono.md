# Urological System Diseases

**泌尿系統超音波**

## Introduction

The urological system encompasses the kidneys, ureters, urinary bladder, urethra, prostate (in males), and the male reproductive organs (testes, epididymis, seminal vesicles). Adrenal glands, though endocrine organs, are anatomically intimately associated with the upper poles of the kidneys and are typically evaluated together.

Urological diseases represent a wide clinical spectrum — from common conditions such as nephrolithiasis and urinary tract infection, to life-threatening emergencies such as renal vein thrombosis, ureteral obstruction, and testicular torsion, to major malignancies including renal cell carcinoma, urothelial carcinoma, and prostate carcinoma.

Ultrasound (US) is the primary first-line imaging modality for most urological conditions due to its safety, availability, and real-time capability. It is particularly powerful for detecting hydronephrosis, renal calculi, bladder lesions, prostatic enlargement, and scrotal pathology. Transabdominal US is complemented by transrectal US (TRUS), Doppler assessment, and contrast-enhanced US (CEUS) where indicated.

**Normal anatomical reference values:**

| Structure                   | Normal US Measurement              |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Kidney length               | 9–12 cm (±1 cm between sides)      |
| Renal cortical thickness    | ≥7 mm                              |
| Renal parenchymal thickness | ≥15 mm                             |
| Renal cortex echogenicity   | Equal to or less than liver/spleen |
| Renal resistive index (RI)  | 0.50–0.70 (adult)                  |
| Ureter diameter             | <3 mm (not normally visualized)    |
| Bladder wall thickness      | ≤3 mm (when full, >300 mL)         |
| Prostate volume             | <30 mL (normal); >30 mL (enlarged) |

## Kidney Diseases

### Normal Kidney Anatomy on Ultrasound

The normal kidney has an echogenic central renal sinus (collecting system and fat) surrounded by hypoechoic renal parenchyma. The cortex is finely granular, hypoechoic relative to renal sinus, and isoechoic or slightly less echogenic than liver. Medullary pyramids appear as hypoechoic triangular structures arranged radially around the central sinus. The renal capsule appears as a thin echogenic line.

### Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

**Causes:** Pre-renal (volume depletion, hemodynamic), intrinsic renal (acute tubular necrosis, glomerulonephritis, interstitial nephritis), post-renal (obstruction)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                    | Findings                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Pre-renal**                | Normal or mildly reduced kidney size; increased RI (>0.70) on Doppler                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Intrinsic renal (ATN/GN)** | Normal or enlarged kidneys; increased cortical echogenicity; loss of corticomedullary differentiation; medullary pyramids may appear prominent (edematous); elevated RI (>0.70 suggests intrinsic disease; >0.80 correlates with poor prognosis) |
| **Acute cortical necrosis**  | Markedly echogenic cortex; rim sign — a thin peripheral echogenic rim (viable subcapsular cortex) with absent inner cortical flow on Doppler                                                                                                     |
| **Post-renal**               | Hydronephrosis; bilateral or solitary functioning kidney obstruction                                                                                                                                                                             |

### Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                 | Findings                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Size/echogenicity**     | Bilaterally small, echogenic kidneys (hallmark of CKD)                                                                                                                    |
| **CMD**                   | Loss of corticomedullary differentiation (cortex and medulla become indistinguishable)                                                                                    |
| **Cortical thickness**    | Cortical thinning (<7 mm)                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Parenchymal thickness** | Reduced overall parenchymal thickness                                                                                                                                     |
| **Surface contour**       | Smooth or slightly irregular surface contour                                                                                                                              |
| **Renal RI**              | Elevated renal RI (>0.70)                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Cysts**                 | Cysts may coexist (acquired cystic kidney disease in dialysis patients: multiple bilateral cortical cysts)                                                                |
| **Special notes**         | Note: In diabetic nephropathy, kidneys may initially appear normal or enlarged before becoming atrophic; HIV nephropathy produces bilaterally enlarged, echogenic kidneys |

### Glomerulonephritis (GN)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter   | Findings                                                                                                                                        |
| ----------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Acute**   | Acutely: Enlarged kidneys, markedly increased cortical echogenicity (brighter than liver), prominent hypoechoic pyramids (medullary congestion) |
| **Cortex**  | Echogenic cortex with preserved or enhanced corticomedullary distinction ("bright cortex-dark medulla" pattern)                                 |
| **RI**      | Elevated RI reflecting increased vascular resistance                                                                                            |
| **Chronic** | Chronically: Progressive atrophy and cortical thinning with scarring                                                                            |

### Renal Cysts

**Simple Renal Cyst (Bosniak Category I)**

The most common renal mass, increasing in prevalence with age.

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter              | Findings                              |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Echogenicity**       | Anechoic, well-defined, round or oval |
| **Wall**               | Thin imperceptible wall (<1 mm)       |
| **Posterior acoustic** | Posterior acoustic enhancement        |
| **Lateral edge**       | Lateral edge (refraction) shadowing   |
| **Doppler**            | No internal vascularity on Doppler    |

**Bosniak Classification of Renal Cysts (CT-based; US used as adjunct):**

| Category | Features                                                        | Malignancy Risk | Management          |
| -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------- | ------------------- |
| I        | Simple cyst; no septa, calcification, or solid component        | ~0%             | No follow-up        |
| II       | Few thin septa; fine calcification; high-density cyst <3 cm     | <5%             | No follow-up        |
| IIF      | Multiple thin septa; moderate calcification; >3 cm high-density | ~5–10%          | Follow-up imaging   |
| III      | Thickened irregular septa/wall; indeterminate                   | ~50%            | Surgical evaluation |
| IV       | Solid enhancing component                                       | >90%            | Surgical resection  |

**Ultrasound features of complex cysts:**

- Septa: thin (<1 mm) or thick (>1 mm); vascularity in septa raises concern
- Calcification: echogenic foci with shadowing
- Solid components: vascular nodules within the cyst

**Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease (ADPKD)**

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                  | Findings                                                                              |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Kidneys**                | Bilaterally massively enlarged kidneys replaced by innumerable cysts of varying sizes |
| **Parenchyma**             | Normal renal parenchyma progressively lost                                            |
| **Volume**                 | Total kidney volume correlates with disease progression                               |
| **Hepatic cysts**          | Associated hepatic cysts (in up to 90% of patients)                                   |
| **Intracranial aneurysms** | Intracranial aneurysms (not US-detectable; screen with MRA)                           |
| **Complicated cysts**      | Complicated cysts: hemorrhagic (hyperechoic content), infected (internal debris)      |

### Renal Calculi (Nephrolithiasis)

**Prevalence:** One of the most common urological conditions; lifetime risk ~10–15%

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter              | Findings                                                                                                                                                   |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Stone**              | Echogenic focus within the renal collecting system (calyx, renal pelvis) with posterior acoustic shadowing                                                 |
| **Twinkling artifact** | Twinkling artifact (color Doppler): rapidly alternating red-blue signal posterior to the stone; highly sensitive for calculi even when shadowing is absent |
| **Hydronephrosis**     | Secondary hydronephrosis (dilation of collecting system proximal to obstruction)                                                                           |
| **Renal RI**           | Elevated renal RI on the obstructed side (>0.70 suggests significant obstruction)                                                                          |
| **Ureteral jet**       | Ureteral jet assessment: absent or reduced ureteral jet on the obstructed side (assessed in the bladder on color Doppler)                                  |

**Limitations:** US sensitivity for ureteral stones is low (~45%); CT KUB remains the gold standard.

### Renal Infections

#### Acute Pyelonephritis

**Ultrasound findings (US may be normal in uncomplicated cases):**

| Parameter           | Findings                                                                                        |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Kidney size**     | Renal enlargement (focal or global)                                                             |
| **Cortex**          | Focal or diffuse cortical hypoechogenicity (edema)                                              |
| **CMD**             | Loss of corticomedullary differentiation in affected segments                                   |
| **Doppler**         | Reduced or absent parenchymal Doppler flow in the affected area (wedge-shaped perfusion defect) |
| **Perinephric fat** | Thickening of perinephric fat (echogenic stranding)                                             |

**Complications:**

**Renal abscess:**

- Round or oval, thick-walled, complex cystic lesion
- Internal echoes, debris, septa, or gas (hyperechoic foci with dirty shadowing)
- Peripheral Doppler vascularity; no internal flow
- May extend into the perinephric space

**Emphysematous pyelonephritis (EPN):**

- Intrarenal gas: hyperechoic foci with dirty shadowing or ring-down artifact
- Shadowing from the renal fossa (obscures normal architecture)
- Surgical/radiological emergency; more common in diabetics

**Perinephric abscess:**

- Complex fluid collection in the perinephric space
- Thickened, irregular walls; internal echoes
- May track along fascial planes

#### Xanthogranulomatous Pyelonephritis (XGP)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                 | Findings                                                                                        |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Kidney**                | Enlarged, non-functioning kidney with central echogenic calculus (staghorn calculus is classic) |
| **Calyces**               | Dilated calyces filled with echogenic material (pus/debris) replacing normal parenchyma         |
| **Perinephric extension** | Perinephric extension: ill-defined echogenic fat with hypoechoic fluid collections              |
| **Bear paw sign**         | "Bear paw" sign: dilated calyces radiating from central calculus                                |

### Renal Tuberculosis

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                 | Findings                                                                                                                   |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Early**                 | Early: Non-specific focal echogenic lesions                                                                                |
| **Calcification**         | Calcification: punctate or curvilinear hyperechoic foci with shadowing; "putty kidney" (complete dystrophic calcification) |
| **Cavitation**            | Parenchymal cavitation: irregular, complex cystic areas within the parenchyma                                              |
| **Hydronephrosis**        | Hydronephrosis due to ureteral stricture                                                                                   |
| **Infundibular stenosis** | Infundibular stenosis: calyceal dilation without overall pelviureteral dilation                                            |
| **Autonephrectomy**       | Autonephrectomy: small, densely calcified, non-functioning kidney                                                          |

### Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC)

**Most common malignant renal tumor in adults (clear cell ~75%, papillary, chromophobe)**

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter              | Findings                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Solid renal mass**   | Variable echogenicity (isoechoic to parenchyma — most common, may be subtle; hyperechoic: small <3 cm clear cell RCC, can mimic angiomyolipoma; hypoechoic: less common; heterogeneous: cystic necrosis, hemorrhage within large tumors) |
| **Renal contour**      | Disruption or expansion of the renal contour                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Doppler**            | Internal vascularity on Doppler (arterial, low-resistance pattern)                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Calcification**      | Calcification possible: irregular or amorphous                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Cystic RCC**         | Cystic RCC: complex cystic mass (Bosniak III–IV)                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| **IVC tumor thrombus** | IVC tumor thrombus: Echogenic intraluminal material extending from renal vein into IVC; vascular on Doppler; may extend to right atrium in advanced cases                                                                                |
| **Advanced disease**   | Renal vein thrombosis, retroperitoneal adenopathy, liver metastases                                                                                                                                                                      |

**Angiomyolipoma (AML):**

| Parameter       | Findings                                                                                           |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Composition** | Benign; composed of blood vessels, smooth muscle, fat                                              |
| **Classic**     | Classic: Uniformly hyperechoic mass (macroscopic fat) with posterior acoustic shadowing            |
| **Small AML**   | Small hyperechoic AML (<3 cm) indistinguishable from small RCC on US — CT/MRI for fat confirmation |
| **Large AML**   | Large AML (>4 cm): risk of spontaneous hemorrhage (Wunderlich syndrome)                            |

**Oncocytoma:**

| Parameter   | Findings                                                              |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Benign**  | Benign; cannot reliably be distinguished from RCC on US               |
| **Doppler** | May show spoke-wheel Doppler pattern (central scar), but not specific |

### Wilms Tumor (Nephroblastoma)

**Most common renal tumor in children (peak age 3–4 years)**

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter          | Findings                                                                               |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Mass**           | Large, solid, well-defined intrarenal mass                                             |
| **Echogenicity**   | Heterogeneous echogenicity (mixed solid and cystic areas with hemorrhage and necrosis) |
| **Growth pattern** | Displaces rather than invades adjacent structures (unlike neuroblastoma)               |
| **Bilaterality**   | Bilateral in 5–10% (synchronous or metachronous)                                       |
| **Tumor thrombus** | IVC and renal vein tumor thrombus possible                                             |
| **Assessment**     | Assess contralateral kidney and liver                                                  |

### Renal Transplant

Post-transplant kidney is in the iliac fossa (right lower quadrant more common); easily accessible to US without bowel gas interference.

**Normal transplant US:**

| Parameter        | Findings                                                                |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Parenchyma**   | Well-defined parenchyma; cortical echogenicity similar to native kidney |
| **RI**           | RI 0.60–0.70 (slightly higher than native)                              |
| **CMD**          | Intact corticomedullary differentiation                                 |
| **Ureteral jet** | Ureteral jet visible in bladder                                         |

**Complications:**

| Complication                 | Ultrasound Finding                                                                                   |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Acute rejection              | Enlarged kidney; increased cortical echogenicity; loss of CMD; elevated RI (>0.80); swollen pyramids |
| Acute tubular necrosis (ATN) | Similar to rejection; clinical history distinguishes                                                 |
| Renal vein thrombosis        | Absent venous Doppler; reversed diastolic arterial flow (RI = 1.0)                                   |
| Renal artery stenosis        | PSV >200 cm/s at stenosis; Tardus-parvus waveform distally                                           |
| Urinoma                      | Anechoic peritransplant collection; adjacent to lower pole or ureterovesical junction                |
| Lymphocele                   | Anechoic/hypoechoic collection with thin septae; no Doppler flow; lateral or inferior to kidney      |
| Hematoma                     | Heterogeneous collection; echogenicity varies with age of hemorrhage                                 |
| Hydronephrosis               | Pelvicalyceal dilation; RI elevation may precede morphological dilation                              |

## Renal Collecting System and Ureter

### Hydronephrosis

**Definition:** Dilation of the renal pelvis and calyces due to obstruction or non-obstructive causes (VUR, pregnancy, overhydration).

**Grading (SFU system):**

| Grade | US Appearance                                                |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 0     | No dilation                                                  |
| 1     | Renal pelvis only mildly dilated; calyces normal             |
| 2     | Pelvis and few calyces dilated                               |
| 3     | Pelvis and all calyces dilated; minimal parenchymal thinning |
| 4     | Gross dilation; significant parenchymal thinning             |

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter             | Findings                                                            |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Collecting system** | Anechoic, interconnected fluid-filled structures in the renal sinus |
| **Renal sinus**       | Loss of the echogenic renal sinus central complex in severe cases   |
| **Cortex**            | Cortical thinning with high-grade chronic obstruction               |
| **RI**                | Elevated renal RI on obstructed side                                |
| **Ureteral jet**      | Absent or reduced ureteral jet on color Doppler in bladder          |

**Causes by level:**

| Parameter                         | Findings                                                                                             |
| --------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **UPJ obstruction**               | Dilation of pelvis and calyces without ureteral dilation                                             |
| **Ureteral stone**                | Proximal ureteral dilation; stone visible as echogenic focus with shadowing and twinkling artifact   |
| **VUJ obstruction / ureterocele** | Dilated ureter down to the bladder; ureterocele visible as thin-walled intravesical cystic structure |

### Ureterocele

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter            | Findings                                                                                              |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Cystic structure** | Thin-walled, cystic structure within the bladder at the ureteral orifice                              |
| **Hydronephrosis**   | Associated hydronephrosis and hydroureter (especially with ectopic ureterocele in duplicated systems) |
| **Cobra head**       | Cobra head appearance on CT urography                                                                 |

### Urothelial Carcinoma of the Ureter

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter            | Findings                                                                                                    |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hydroureter**      | Hydroureter proximal to an intraluminal mass                                                                |
| **Filling defect**   | Soft tissue echogenic filling defect within the ureter (often not directly visualized on transabdominal US) |
| **Hydronephrosis**   | Proximal hydronephrosis                                                                                     |
| **Primary modality** | CT urography is the primary imaging modality; US is supportive                                              |

## Urinary Bladder Diseases

**Technique:** Examine with full bladder (>300 mL). Measure post-void residual (PVR) for bladder emptying assessment.

**Normal bladder wall:** ≤3 mm when full; ≤5 mm when empty.

### Cystitis (Bladder Infection)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                  | Findings                                                                                                                                     |
| -------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Wall**                   | Diffuse bladder wall thickening (>3 mm when full)                                                                                            |
| **Lumen**                  | Echogenic debris or mobile low-level echoes within the bladder lumen (pyuria, hematuria)                                                     |
| **Capacity**               | Reduced bladder capacity                                                                                                                     |
| **Emphysematous cystitis** | Emphysematous cystitis (diabetics, immunocompromised): intramural gas producing hyperechoic foci with dirty shadowing and ring-down artifact |

### Bladder Calculi

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter              | Findings                                                                                      |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Stone**              | Mobile echogenic foci with posterior acoustic shadowing                                       |
| **Gravity dependence** | Gravity-dependent: shifts with patient repositioning (differentiates from fixed mural lesion) |
| **Twinkling artifact** | Twinkling artifact on color Doppler                                                           |
| **Number/cause**       | May be single or multiple; associated with bladder outlet obstruction or foreign body         |

### Bladder Carcinoma (Urothelial Carcinoma)

**Most common bladder malignancy (~90% urothelial carcinoma)**

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                | Findings                                                                                                            |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Papillary/polypoid**   | Papillary/polypoid: Echogenic, fixed (non-mobile), irregular intraluminal mass with internal vascularity on Doppler |
| **Sessile/flat**         | Sessile/flat: Focal or diffuse mural thickening; irregular mucosal surface                                          |
| **Muscle-invasive**      | Muscle-invasive disease: Loss of normal bladder wall layer echogenicity; irregular outer bladder contour            |
| **Perivesical invasion** | Bladder wall disruption with perivesical fat invasion                                                               |
| **Hydronephrosis**       | Hydronephrosis if ureteral orifice involved                                                                         |
| **LN**                   | Pelvic lymphadenopathy                                                                                              |
| **Limitation**           | Limitation: Small, flat (CIS) lesions may not be visualized; cystoscopy remains gold standard                       |

### Bladder Outlet Obstruction (BOO)

**Causes:** BPH (most common in males), urethral stricture, bladder neck stenosis, posterior urethral valves (pediatric)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter          | Findings                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Trabeculation**  | Bladder wall trabeculation: irregular, thickened wall with coarse internal projections                                                       |
| **Diverticula**    | Bladder diverticula: outpouchings through gaps in the detrusor; rounded anechoic structures adjacent to the bladder; no internal vascularity |
| **PVR**            | Elevated post-void residual (PVR): >100 mL (significant); >300 mL (severe)                                                                   |
| **Hydronephrosis** | Bilateral hydronephrosis (if prolonged/severe)                                                                                               |
| **Prostate**       | Enlarged prostate on transabdominal US                                                                                                       |

**Post-void residual calculation:**

> PVR (mL) = 0.52 × AP diameter × transverse diameter × craniocaudal diameter

### Vesicoureteral Reflux (VUR)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter         | Findings                                                                                     |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Resting US**    | May be normal on resting US                                                                  |
| **Voiding**       | Intermittent ureteral and pelvicalyceal dilation during voiding                              |
| **Gold standard** | Voiding cystourethrogram (VCUG) remains the gold standard                                    |
| **Scarring**      | Renal scarring in chronic VUR: focal cortical thinning, echogenic scars, reduced kidney size |

## Prostate Diseases

**Technique:** Transabdominal US (TAUS) for overall volume; transrectal US (TRUS) for detailed evaluation and biopsy guidance.

**Prostate volume = 0.52 × AP × transverse × craniocaudal diameter**

**Zonal anatomy (McNeal):**

- Peripheral zone (PZ): ~70% of glandular tissue; most prostate cancers arise here
- Transition zone (TZ): surrounds the proximal urethra; site of BPH
- Central zone (CZ): surrounds ejaculatory ducts
- Fibromuscular stroma: anterior non-glandular tissue

### Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)

**Most common cause of lower urinary tract symptoms (LUTS) in men >50 years**

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter           | Findings                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Size**            | Enlarged prostate (volume >30 mL; severe >80 mL)                                                                                                                |
| **Transition zone** | Transition zone hypertrophy: heterogeneous, nodular TZ enlarging toward the urethra                                                                             |
| **IPP**             | Intravesical prostatic protrusion (IPP): prostatic tissue projecting into the bladder lumen; IPP >10 mm correlates with high PVR and bladder outlet obstruction |
| **Bladder**         | Bladder changes: trabeculation, diverticula, elevated PVR                                                                                                       |
| **TRUS**            | TRUS: heterogeneous TZ with multiple echogenic and hypoechoic nodules (adenomatous and fibromuscular nodules)                                                   |
| **Calcification**   | Calcification: echogenic foci at the surgical capsule (separates TZ from PZ)                                                                                    |
| **Hydronephrosis**  | Bilateral hydronephrosis in severe, prolonged obstruction                                                                                                       |

### Prostatitis

#### Acute Bacterial Prostatitis

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter             | Findings                                                        |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Size**              | Enlarged, heterogeneous prostate                                |
| **Echogenicity**      | Hypoechoic areas (edema, inflammation) in the peripheral zone   |
| **Doppler**           | Increased vascularity on color/power Doppler (diffuse or focal) |
| **Periprostatic fat** | Periprostatic fat stranding                                     |
| **Seminal vesicles**  | Seminal vesicle enlargement                                     |

#### Prostatic Abscess

**Ultrasound findings (TRUS is superior):**

| Parameter      | Findings                                                                               |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Collection** | Hypoechoic or complex cystic collection within the prostate                            |
| **Walls**      | Thick, irregular walls; internal debris                                                |
| **Doppler**    | Peripheral Doppler hyperemia; no internal flow                                         |
| **Loculation** | May be multiloculated                                                                  |
| **Clinical**   | More common in diabetics, immunocompromised, or inadequately treated acute prostatitis |

#### Chronic Prostatitis / Chronic Pelvic Pain Syndrome

**Ultrasound findings (often nonspecific):**

| Parameter            | Findings                                                                                     |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Echotexture**      | Heterogeneous prostatic echotexture                                                          |
| **Calcifications**   | Calcifications: grouped echogenic foci (calculi) in the periurethral or intraprostatic ducts |
| **Seminal vesicles** | Seminal vesicle dilation or asymmetry                                                        |
| **Normal**           | May appear entirely normal                                                                   |

### Prostate Carcinoma

**Most common male malignancy; androgen-dependent; majority are adenocarcinoma**

**Ultrasound (TRUS) findings:**

| Parameter         | Findings                                                                                             |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Classic**       | Classic: Hypoechoic nodule in the peripheral zone (sensitivity ~60–70%; many cancers are isoechoic)  |
| **Isoechoic**     | Isoechoic lesions (>30%): indistinguishable from background; detected only on systematic biopsy      |
| **PZ asymmetry**  | Asymmetric peripheral zone echogenicity                                                              |
| **Extracapsular** | Capsular irregularity or bulging (extracapsular extension)                                           |
| **SV invasion**   | Seminal vesicle invasion: asymmetric enlargement, loss of normal echogenicity                        |
| **Doppler**       | Doppler: focal hypervascularity within a hypoechoic nodule (also seen in prostatitis — non-specific) |
| **LN**            | Lymphadenopathy: iliac and obturator nodes                                                           |

**Seminal vesicle invasion indicators:**

| Parameter                | Findings                                                         |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Angle loss**           | Loss of the echogenic angle between prostate and seminal vesicle |
| **Rectoprostatic angle** | Obliteration of the rectoprostatic angle                         |
| **Asymmetry**            | Asymmetric seminal vesicle echogenicity                          |

**Note:** mpMRI with PI-RADS scoring has largely superseded TRUS for prostate cancer detection; TRUS-guided biopsy remains the standard procedural platform.

## Scrotal and Testicular Diseases

**Technique:** High-frequency linear transducer (10–18 MHz); always compare both sides; always include color/power Doppler. Normal testis: homogeneous, medium echogenicity; normal epididymal head: isoechoic or slightly hyperechoic.

### Testicular Torsion

**Surgical emergency:** Complete torsion results in infarction within 4–6 hours; salvage rate ~90% within 6 hours, ~50% at 12 hours, <10% at 24 hours.

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter               | Findings                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Early (0–6 h)**       | Early (0–6 hours): Testis may appear normal on B-mode; critical finding is absent or markedly reduced testicular blood flow on Doppler compared to the contralateral side |
| **Enlargement**         | Enlarged, heterogeneous testis (reactive edema)                                                                                                                           |
| **Whirlpool sign**      | Whirlpool sign: Twisted, coiled spermatic cord on B-mode or Doppler — high specificity (~99%) for torsion                                                                 |
| **Peritesticular flow** | Absent peritesticular flow while epididymal flow may be retained (partial torsion)                                                                                        |
| **Hydrocele**           | Reactive hydrocele (non-specific)                                                                                                                                         |
| **Late (>6 h)**         | Late (>6 hours): Heterogeneous, hypoechoic testis (infarction); absent Doppler flow; scrotal wall thickening                                                              |

**Pitfall:** Spontaneous detorsion may show preserved or even increased Doppler flow (hyperemia); heterogeneous echotexture still suggests prior ischemia.

### Epididymo-Orchitis

**Most common cause of acute scrotal pain in adults post-puberty**

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter              | Findings                                                                                        |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Epididymal size**    | Enlarged epididymis (head >12 mm; body/tail involvement in severe cases)                        |
| **Epididymal echo**    | Hypoechoic epididymis (edema)                                                                   |
| **Epididymal Doppler** | Markedly increased epididymal vascularity on Doppler (key distinguishing feature from torsion)  |
| **Orchitis**           | Orchitis: enlarged, hypoechoic testis with increased testicular flow                            |
| **Hydrocele**          | Reactive hydrocele                                                                              |
| **Scrotal skin**       | Scrotal skin thickening                                                                         |
| **Pyocele**            | Pyocele: echogenic/complex fluid in the scrotal sac (pus)                                       |
| **Abscess**            | Epididymal abscess: complex hypoechoic collection with peripheral vascularity; no internal flow |

### Hydrocele

**Types:** Primary (idiopathic), secondary (reactive to orchitis, tumor, trauma)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter             | Findings                                                                           |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fluid**             | Anechoic fluid surrounding the testis and epididymis                               |
| **Simple**            | Simple: Anechoic, no internal echoes or septations                                 |
| **Complex**           | Complex: Echogenic debris (hematocele, pyocele), septations (chronic/inflammatory) |
| **Underlying testis** | Always assess underlying testis carefully to exclude tumor as the cause            |

### Varicocele

**Dilated pampiniform plexus veins; ~15% of males; associated with male infertility**

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter       | Findings                                                                                                                       |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Veins**       | Multiple serpiginous anechoic tubular structures posterior and superior to the testis (diameter >2–3 mm; >3 mm is significant) |
| **Laterality**  | Left-sided predominance (90%)                                                                                                  |
| **Valsalva**    | Augmentation on Valsalva maneuver: veins dilate; Doppler shows retrograde venous flow                                          |
| **Grading**     | Grading: Grade I (Valsalva only) → Grade II (palpable at rest) → Grade III (visible at rest)                                   |
| **Right-sided** | Right-sided or isolated varicocele: assess retroperitoneum for mass or IVC obstruction                                         |

### Testicular Microlithiasis

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter        | Findings                                                                                                                                                                |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Foci**         | Punctate hyperechoic foci (<3 mm) within the testicular parenchyma, diffusely distributed                                                                               |
| **Classic**      | Classic pattern: ≥5 foci per transducer view; no posterior acoustic shadowing                                                                                           |
| **Limited**      | Limited pattern: <5 foci per view                                                                                                                                       |
| **Significance** | Association with testicular germ cell tumor debated; annual surveillance US recommended with risk factors (history of GCT, cryptorchidism, infertility, family history) |

### Testicular Tumors

**Germ cell tumors (GCT, ~95%):** Seminoma (most common, ~50%); non-seminomatous GCT (embryonal, yolk sac, choriocarcinoma, teratoma)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                | Findings                                                                                                                                |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Rule**                 | Any solid intratesticular lesion should be presumed malignant until proven otherwise                                                    |
| **Seminoma**             | Seminoma: Well-defined, homogeneous, hypoechoic mass; lobulated margins; internal vascularity on Doppler; rarely calcifies              |
| **Non-seminomatous GCT** | Non-seminomatous GCT: Heterogeneous, mixed echogenicity; calcification, cystic areas, hemorrhage common                                 |
| **Burned-out tumor**     | Burned-out tumor (regressed primary): Focal echogenic scar with calcification; retroperitoneal metastases may be the presenting finding |
| **Lymphoma**             | Lymphoma (secondary): Older males; bilateral involvement; hypoechoic with diffuse infiltration pattern; increased Doppler flow          |
| **Metastases**           | Assess retroperitoneal lymph nodes and liver                                                                                            |

### Epididymal Cyst and Spermatocele

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter           | Findings                                                                                                                           |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Epididymal cyst** | Epididymal cyst: Anechoic, well-defined, thin-walled; located in the epididymis (usually head); no internal echoes; benign         |
| **Spermatocele**    | Spermatocele: Similar appearance; may contain low-level echoes (spermatozoa); located in the epididymal head; often larger; benign |

### Scrotal Trauma

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter    | Findings                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Rupture**  | Testicular rupture: Irregular, poorly defined testicular contour; disruption of the tunica albuginea (hyperechoic line); heterogeneous echotexture (hemorrhage, infarction); hematocele (complex echogenic scrotal fluid) |
| **Hematoma** | Hematoma: Heterogeneous extra- or intratesticular collection; echogenicity evolves with age (acute: echogenic; subacute: mixed; chronic: hypoechoic)                                                                      |
| **Doppler**  | Doppler: Reduced or absent flow indicates vascular compromise (surgical exploration required)                                                                                                                             |

## Adrenal Gland Diseases

**Normal adrenal gland:** Inverted Y- or V-shaped; right adrenal is superomedial to the right kidney; left is anteromedial to the left upper pole. Normal limb thickness <10 mm.

### Adrenal Adenoma

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter            | Findings                                                                                                                                   |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Mass**             | Oval, well-defined, homogeneous hypoechoic or isoechoic mass                                                                               |
| **Size**             | Small (<3 cm), smooth margins                                                                                                              |
| **Characterization** | US cannot reliably characterize adenoma vs. other adrenal masses — CT with washout protocol or MRI with chemical shift imaging is required |
| **Incidentaloma**    | Incidentaloma: adrenal mass found incidentally; requires biochemical evaluation and CT characterization                                    |

### Adrenocortical Carcinoma (ACC)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter         | Findings                                                                                    |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Size**          | Large (often >6 cm), heterogeneous mass                                                     |
| **Echogenicity**  | Mixed echogenicity with cystic necrosis and calcification (hyperechoic foci with shadowing) |
| **Margins**       | Irregular margins; invasion of adjacent structures                                          |
| **IVC extension** | IVC extension (tumor thrombus): echogenic material in IVC                                   |
| **Metastases**    | Retroperitoneal lymphadenopathy, liver metastases                                           |

### Pheochromocytoma

**Clinical:** Episodic hypertension, headache, diaphoresis, palpitations; rule of 10s (10% bilateral, 10% malignant, 10% extra-adrenal)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter          | Findings                                                                                                                 |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Mass**           | Round or oval, well-defined mass; variable size (2–10+ cm)                                                               |
| **Echogenicity**   | Heterogeneous echogenicity: solid with cystic/necrotic areas                                                             |
| **Doppler**        | Marked internal vascularity on Doppler                                                                                   |
| **Calcifications** | May have calcifications                                                                                                  |
| **Extra-adrenal**  | Extra-adrenal paraganglioma: along the sympathetic chain, organ of Zuckerkandl (at the aortic bifurcation), bladder wall |

### Adrenal Hemorrhage

**Causes:** Neonatal stress, trauma, sepsis (Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome), anticoagulation

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter    | Findings                                                                                                                                            |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Acute**    | Acutely (first week): Hyperechoic mass (acute hemorrhage)                                                                                           |
| **Subacute** | Subacutely (1–4 weeks): Becomes hypoechoic/complex as clot liquefies                                                                                |
| **Chronic**  | Chronically (>1 month): Well-defined, anechoic cystic collection; may calcify (peripheral eggshell calcification)                                   |
| **Neonatal** | Neonatal: suprarenal echogenic mass, right > left; kidney displaced downward (differentiates from neuroblastoma, which displaces kidney anteriorly) |

### Adrenal Metastasis

**Most common primary sites:** Lung (most common), breast, melanoma, colon, renal

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter        | Findings                                                      |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bilaterality** | Bilateral adrenal masses more common than with primary tumors |
| **Echogenicity** | Heterogeneous, variable size; may have necrotic centers       |
| **Modality**     | CT/PET-CT for characterization                                |

## Renovascular Diseases

### Renal Artery Stenosis (RAS)

**Causes:** Atherosclerosis (proximal, ostial; elderly), fibromuscular dysplasia (mid/distal artery; young women)

**Doppler criteria:**

**Direct criteria (at the stenosis):**

| Parameter         | Findings                                                   |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **PSV**           | Peak systolic velocity (PSV) >180–200 cm/s at the stenosis |
| **RAR**           | Renal artery-to-aortic ratio (RAR) >3.5                    |
| **Color Doppler** | Turbulent/mosaic color Doppler pattern at the stenosis     |
| **Aliasing**      | Aliasing artifact on color Doppler                         |

**Indirect criteria (intrarenal waveform):**

| Parameter         | Findings                                                                                                     |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Tardus-parvus** | Tardus-parvus waveform: delayed systolic upstroke (acceleration time >70–100 ms; acceleration index <3 m/s²) |
| **RI**            | Reduced RI (<0.50 in the affected kidney; asymmetry >0.05 between kidneys suggests upstream stenosis)        |
| **Systolic peak** | Blunted, rounded systolic peak                                                                               |

**Secondary findings:**

| Parameter          | Findings                                  |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------- |
| **Kidney atrophy** | Ipsilateral kidney atrophy (chronic RAS)  |
| **Size asymmetry** | Asymmetric kidney size (>2 cm difference) |

### Renal Vein Thrombosis (RVT)

**Causes:** Nephrotic syndrome, hypercoagulable state, renal cell carcinoma (tumor thrombus), trauma, dehydration (neonates)

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter            | Findings                                                                                           |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Thrombus**         | Echogenic material within the renal vein (acute thrombus may be hypoechoic/anechoic)               |
| **Venous Doppler**   | Absent or reversed renal venous Doppler signal                                                     |
| **Arterial Doppler** | Reversed diastolic arterial flow on intrarenal Doppler (RI approaching or equaling 1.0)            |
| **Kidney**           | Enlarged, edematous kidney (acute phase)                                                           |
| **Collaterals**      | Collateral venous pathways (chronic)                                                               |
| **Tumor thrombus**   | In tumor thrombus: arterial Doppler signal within the thrombus (distinguishes from bland thrombus) |

### Renal Artery Aneurysm

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter         | Findings                                                                             |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Outpouching**   | Focal outpouching from the renal artery; may appear as a pulsatile perivascular mass |
| **Echogenicity**  | Anechoic or heterogeneous (mural thrombus)                                           |
| **Color Doppler** | Color Doppler: "yin-yang" sign (bidirectional swirling flow)                         |
| **Waveform**      | Doppler waveform: turbulent, high-velocity flow within the aneurysm                  |

### Arteriovenous Fistula / Malformation (AVF/AVM)

**Causes:** Post-biopsy (most common iatrogenic cause), trauma, tumor

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter             | Findings                                                     |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Color Doppler**     | Color Doppler: Focal area of color aliasing (turbulent flow) |
| **Venous flow**       | Pulsatile venous flow in the feeding vein (arterialization)  |
| **Arterial waveform** | Low-resistance arterial waveform in the feeding artery       |
| **Tissue vibration**  | Perivascular tissue vibration artifact (color "bruit")       |
| **Prognosis**         | Most post-biopsy AVFs resolve spontaneously within 6 months  |

## Urological Emergencies and POCUS Applications

### Point-of-Care Ultrasound (POCUS) in Urology

POCUS enables rapid bedside assessment in emergency and clinical settings.

#### Renal Colic — Rapid Assessment Protocol

1. **Assess for hydronephrosis** (bilateral views, longitudinal and transverse)
2. **Bladder assessment:** Measure PVR; assess for intravesical stone; evaluate ureteral jets bilaterally on color Doppler
3. **Look for stone:** Renal pelvis and UPJ; distal ureter at the VUJ
4. **Measure renal RI** if hydronephrosis present (RI >0.70 on obstructed side supports significant obstruction)
5. **Check for aortic aneurysm** — critical in older patients presenting with flank pain; do not assume a ureteral stone

#### Urological POCUS Indications

| Indication                  | Key Assessment                                        |
| --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Acute scrotal pain          | Testicular Doppler — torsion vs. epididymo-orchitis   |
| Renal colic                 | Hydronephrosis, stone, ureteral jet, RI               |
| Urinary retention           | Bladder volume, PVR, prostate assessment              |
| Hematuria                   | Bladder mass, renal mass, ureteral dilation           |
| Trauma (FAST)               | Free fluid in pelvis, bladder integrity               |
| Sepsis (urological source)  | Hydronephrosis, renal abscess, perinephric collection |
| Renal transplant monitoring | RI, venous flow, collecting system dilation           |

### Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (AAA) — Urological Mimic

**Critical:** Ruptured or leaking AAA presents with flank/back pain identical to renal colic; must be excluded in any male >55 years with sudden-onset flank pain.

**Ultrasound findings:**

| Parameter                 | Findings                                                                                                      |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Diameter**              | Transverse aortic diameter ≥3 cm = aneurysm (normal: <2.5 cm)                                                 |
| **Measurement**           | Anteroposterior measurement (outer-to-outer wall) in the transverse plane is most reproducible                |
| **Intraluminal thrombus** | Intraluminal thrombus: echogenic crescentic material lining the aneurysm wall                                 |
| **Hematoma**              | Periaortic hematoma (leaking/ruptured AAA): heterogeneous retroperitoneal tissue obscuring the aortic margins |
| **Stability**             | Hemodynamic instability: do not delay for extensive imaging — immediate surgical consultation                 |

## Summary of Key Ultrasound Signs

| Sign                                    | Description                                                               | Disease                                   |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Twinkling artifact                      | Rapidly alternating color Doppler signal behind echogenic foci            | Renal/bladder calculi                     |
| Whirlpool sign                          | Coiled, twisted spermatic cord on Doppler                                 | Testicular torsion                        |
| Tardus-parvus waveform                  | Delayed systolic upstroke; blunted peak in intrarenal arteries            | Renal artery stenosis (downstream)        |
| Reversed diastolic flow                 | Diastolic flow reversed in intrarenal arteries                            | Renal vein thrombosis; acute rejection    |
| Rim sign (cortical necrosis)            | Thin echogenic subcapsular rim; absent cortical Doppler flow              | Acute cortical necrosis                   |
| Ureteral jet                            | Color Doppler puff of urine into bladder                                  | Absent/reduced = obstruction on that side |
| IPP (intravesical prostatic protrusion) | Prostate tissue projecting into bladder lumen >10 mm                      | Severe BPH with BOO                       |
| Acoustic shadowing                      | No-signal zone posterior to calcified/dense structure                     | Calculi, calcification                    |
| Posterior enhancement                   | Increased signal distal to fluid-filled structure                         | Simple cyst, hydrocele, urinoma           |
| Yin-yang sign                           | Bidirectional turbulent Doppler flow                                      | Pseudoaneurysm, AV fistula                |
| Bear paw sign                           | Dilated calyces radiating from central staghorn calculus                  | Xanthogranulomatous pyelonephritis        |
| Spoke-wheel Doppler                     | Radiating arterial vessels from central point                             | Oncocytoma                                |
| Basket pattern Doppler                  | Peripheral vascularity around renal mass                                  | Renal cell carcinoma                      |
| Tumor thrombus (IVC)                    | Echogenic arterially vascular intraluminal material in IVC                | RCC, ACC, Wilms tumor                     |
| Peritesticular hypervascularity         | Increased epididymal/scrotal flow; preserved or increased testicular flow | Epididymo-orchitis                        |
| Absent testicular flow                  | No Doppler signal in testis vs. contralateral                             | Testicular torsion (surgical emergency)   |
| Bosniak I cyst features                 | Anechoic, thin wall, posterior enhancement, no vascularity                | Simple benign renal cyst                  |
| Bright cortex-dark medulla              | Hyperechoic cortex with prominent hypoechoic pyramids                     | Acute glomerulonephritis                  |
