Prolotherapy for Shoulder / Supraspinatus
Mechanism
Inject a proliferant (typically hypertonic dextrose) at tendon/ligament entheses → local inflammation → growth factor recruitment → collagen remodeling. Not instant; requires 3–6 sessions.
Typical protocol (supraspinatus insertion)
Step	Detail
Solution	12.5–25% dextrose (1 part D50 + 1–3 parts 1% lidocaine or saline)
Volume	1–3 mL total per session
Needle	27G × 1.5" (or 25G for deeper)
Frequency	Every 3–4 weeks × 3–6 sessions
---	---
Technique (US-guided, in-plane approach)
1. Position: Patient seated, hand behind back (supraspinatus exposed anteriorly) or arm adducted across chest.
2. Target: Greater tuberosity enthesis (supraspinatus footprint) + tendon substance where thickened/defective.
3. Inject in 3–4 aliquots (peppering technique):
   - 0.5–1 mL at the bony insertion (periosteal peel)
   - 0.5 mL into the tendon midsubstance (avoid intratendinous high-pressure — painful)
   - 0.5 mL at the bursal surface (subacromial space)
4. Post-injection: Soreness for 24–72 h (expected inflammatory response). Ice, acetaminophen only (avoid NSAIDs — they defeat the purpose).
For DRA (β2M amyloid) specifically
Factor	Consideration
Efficacy	Limited data. Dextrose stimulates collagen, but DRA is amyloid infiltration, not tendinosis — less responsive
Alternative	PRP likely better (growth factors active on degenerate tissue)
Practical	If you try dextrose: 15% × 2–3 mL, same technique; temper expectations
Key precautions (HD patient)
- ✅ Acetaminophen for post-procedure pain
- ❌ Avoid NSAIDs for 7–10 days post-injection (they block the proliferant response — the whole point)
- ✅ Sterile technique mandatory (immunocompromised + fistula)
- ✅ HD timing: inject non-HD day (no heparin overlap, less bleeding)
- ✅ Fistula arm: inject lateral/posterior supraspinatus, avoid medial approach near cephalic vein
- ⚠️ Do not inject if active infection, cellulitis, or bacteremia
Expected timeline
Session	Effect
1–2	Increased soreness, minimal improvement
3–4	Pain starts to reduce
5–6	Max benefit; may repeat q6–12 months PRN
If no response after 4 sessions
→ Reconsider diagnosis: full-thickness tear? glenohumeral arthritis? cervical radiculopathy? DRA severe → transplant evaluation.
